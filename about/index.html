About Frame

Frame is a curated cinema platform dedicated to independent, open-source, and AI-generated short films. It exists to make boundary-pushing filmmaking accessible in one place.

The idea

Short films rarely get the attention they deserve. They live scattered across YouTube channels, Vimeo profiles, and studio archives — buried under algorithmic noise. Frame collects the best of them, organises them by source and theme, and presents them in a lean, distraction-free environment built for watching, not browsing.

The catalog spans studio animation by Blender artists, science-fiction shorts from DUST, character-driven drama from Omeleto, and award-winning AI cinema from the Runway AI Film Festival. Every film is hand-picked.

What makes it different

Most platforms optimise for time-on-site. Frame optimises for a good film. There are no autoplay queues, no forced recommendations mid-playback, and no ads. Progress saves automatically so you can pick up where you left off across sessions.

The film detail page provides full metadata, related picks scored by channel and genre affinity, and a clean player — no distractions, just the film.

Technology

Frame is a static web application — no server-side rendering, no database, no tracking. The catalog lives in a single JSON file. The player is powered by Plyr over YouTube nocookie and Vimeo embeds. It is deployed on Vercel at the edge globally.

HTML / CSS / JS Plyr 3.7.8 JSON-LD structured data YouTube nocookie Vimeo Vercel CDN

Rights & attribution

Frame does not host any video files. All films are streamed from their original sources — YouTube and Vimeo — and remain the property of their respective creators and rights holders. Frame is a nonprofit curatorial project with no commercial intent.

If you are a rights holder and wish to have a film removed, please use the contact information associated with this project.

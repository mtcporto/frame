Runway AI Film Festival

runwayml.com/aiff ↗
28 films

The Runway AI Film Festival (AIFF) is an annual global competition celebrating the intersection of artificial intelligence and filmmaking. Founded by Runway in 2023, it has received thousands of submissions from over 100 countries in its first two editions.

The festival champions a new generation of filmmakers using generative AI tools — including Runway's own Gen-2 and Gen-3 models — to create work that would not have been possible through traditional production pipelines. Films range from intimate character studies to surreal visual experiments.

Frame carries all award-winning and shortlisted films from the 2023, 2024, and 2025 editions.

DUST

youtube.com/@dust ↗
22 films

DUST is the world's largest curated collection of sci-fi short films. Founded in 2016 and owned by Gunpowder & Sky, the channel has amassed over 6 million subscribers and hundreds of original and sourced science-fiction shorts — many of them award-winners from festivals including SXSW, Tribeca, and Cannes.

DUST's philosophy is that science fiction is the most important genre of our time: a space to explore how technology, society, and humanity intersect. Their films explore AI, space, identity, time, and ethical near-futures with the rigour of literary sci-fi.

Frame features a selection of DUST originals and curated favourites spanning 2016 to the present.

Omeleto

youtube.com/@omeleto ↗
20 films

Omeleto is one of the world's leading curators of award-winning short films, with over 6 million YouTube subscribers. Since 2012 they have been licensing and distributing independent films from festival circuits worldwide — Sundance, BAFTA, OSCAR-qualifying festivals and beyond.

Their catalog spans drama, comedy, thriller, and everything in between. What unites them is craft: precision screenwriting, strong performances, and a distinct directorial voice. Omeleto films tell complete stories in 5 to 30 minutes that rival the emotional depth of feature films.

Frame showcases twenty of Omeleto's most celebrated titles, selected for storytelling range and human resonance.

Drama Short Film Festival Circuit Award-winning

Blender Studio & Foundation

studio.blender.org ↗
16 films

Blender Studio (formerly Blender Institute) is the production arm of the Blender Foundation, the nonprofit behind the open-source 3D software Blender. Since 2006, they have produced a series of "open movies" — fully animated short films created entirely with Blender and released under Creative Commons licenses, with all assets freely available to the public.

Each open film serves a dual purpose: to push the boundaries of what Blender can achieve, and to fund its continued development through community subscriptions. Films like Elephants Dream (2006), Big Buck Bunny (2008), Sintel (2010), and Cosmos Laundromat (2015) are milestones in open-source animation history.

Frame carries the complete filmography — from early experiments to contemporary titles like Sprite Fright and Wing It!.
